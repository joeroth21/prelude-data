PRELUDEPrivacy Policy

Effective July 23, 2026 · applies to the PRELUDE iOS app

The short version

PRELUDE has no accounts, no analytics, no ads, and no tracking. The app collects no personal information. Everything you do in the app — your watchlist, alert thresholds, and settings — is stored only on your device and never leaves it.

What the app does on the network

PRELUDE contacts exactly one origin: this data feed (joeroth21.github.io), hosted on GitHub Pages, to download published market data. These are ordinary, read-only web requests — no identifiers, credentials, or personal data are sent. GitHub may record standard server logs (such as IP addresses) when serving any GitHub Pages site; see GitHub's privacy statement.

Purchases

Premium subscriptions are processed by Apple through the App Store. To validate purchases, the app uses RevenueCat, which receives the purchase receipt and a randomly generated anonymous identifier. This is not linked to your name, email, or identity — PRELUDE has no accounts, so there is no identity to link it to. See RevenueCat's privacy policy.

What stays on your device

  • Your watchlist and premium/discount thresholds
  • App settings and alert preferences
  • The cached data feed

Alerts and the weekly digest are computed on your device from the downloaded feed. Notifications are scheduled locally; PRELUDE operates no push servers.

What we never do

  • No accounts or sign-in — we cannot identify you
  • No analytics or crash-reporting SDKs
  • No advertising, no data brokers, no sale of data
  • No access to contacts, photos, camera, or location

Data deletion

Deleting the app deletes all locally stored data. Subscription records held by Apple are governed by your Apple account; RevenueCat's anonymous record can be deleted on request via the contact below.

Changes

If this policy changes, the new version will be posted at this URL with an updated effective date.
